<p>The National Digital Literacy Programme (NDLP) was launched in March 2020
to make digital learning inclusive by equipping students with digital literacies
to be future-ready.</p>
<p>As part of NDLP, MOE announced in 2020 that every secondary school student
will own a school-prescribed personal learning device (PLD) by end-2021.</p>
<p>The aims of NDLP are to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Support the Development of Digital Literacies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Support self-directed and collaborative learning</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Enhance Teaching and Learning</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4>How Will Your Child Be Using Their PLDs?</h4>
<p>They will use their PLDs to access platforms such as the Student Learning
Space (SLS), Google Workspace, Slido, Mentimeter, Kahoot, Nearpod, Padlet,
to:</p>
<p><strong>[In school]</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>To support their learning experiences</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To review their learning and close learning gaps</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To collaborate with their peers and teachers</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>[Beyond school]</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>To continue to learn during home-based learning (HBL)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To explore topics of their personal interests and preferences</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To learn and make use of digital tools such as calendaring and note-taking
applications to enhance personal productivity and develop life skills</p>
</li>
</ul>

</li>

<h4>Cyber Wellness Education</h4>
<p>MOE has enhanced Character and Citizenship Education (CCE 2021) to significantly
incorporate cyber wellness to educate students on the demands of NDLP.</p>
<p>Topics covered in the Cyber Wellness lessons include:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Cyber Use</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Cyber Identity</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Cyber Relationships</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Cyber Citizenship</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Cyber Ethics</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>As parents, you can also play a part by</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>modelling good digital habits for your child,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>knowing your child well, and have conversations with your child about
safe and responsible use of technology,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>setting ground rules for internet use,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>navigating the internet together with your child to understand their usage.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4>Device Management Application (DMA) - Mobile Guardian</h4>
<p>Device management application (DMA) software will be installed on your
child’s device to provide a safe learning experience for your child, and
prevent misuse of the device.</p>
<p>The DMA will be funded by MOE and will be uninstalled from the device
when your child/ward graduates or leaves the school. The purpose of DMA
are three fold as follows:</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Classroom Management</p>
</th>
<th rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Mobile Device Management</p>
</th>
<th rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Usage Management</p>
</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>To enable teachers to actively monitor and control the students' use of
the PLD during lesson time to improve student management and deliver effective
teaching.</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>To enable remote deployment of teaching and learning applications and
security patches.</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>To enable the school to address cyber wellness concerns, such as excessive
and inappropriate use of the PLD and manage prolonged use of the PLD to
reduce stress on the eyes.</p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
